PERSPECTIVE

Some brands are considered trustworthy.

Others…are still finding their way.

Some can claim a higher purpose.

Others…are still reaching.

The difference is often in the creative idea.

Brands can communicate more information to more people

faster than ever before.

But consumers can also learn more about brands—their words

and their actions—in greater detail, depth and speed than ever

before, as well.

There is no place for a brand to hide.

And this has fundamentally changed how creative ideas work.

Ideas that work today are not made of stone—they live and

breathe and change.

Their conversation with the outside world goes beyond send,

to send and receive.

Their point-of-view shifts from brand-centric to the brand’s

role in the lives of consumers.

And their touch points have evolved from promises to deeds.

For brands to claim a higher purpose today, they need to live

among the people—the only place brand trust can thrive.
